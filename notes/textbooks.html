Online Textbooks in Statistics and Probability

(back to index)

 

Some interesting textbooks available online:

  • OpenIntro Statistics
  • Introduction to Statistical Thought by Michael L. Lavine.
  • Probability Theory: The Logic of Science by Edwin T. Jayes.
  • Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms by David J. C. MacKay.
  • The Elements of Statistical Learning (2nd edition) by Trevor Hastie, Robert Tibshirani, and Jerome Friedman
  • An Introduction to Statistical Learning by Gareth James, Daniela Witten, Trevor Hastie and Robert Tibshirani
  • Introduction to Probability by Charles M. Grinstead and J. Laurie Snell.
  • StatProb Encyclopedia
  • The Electronic Statistics Textbook by Thomas Hill and Pawel Lewicki.
  • Advanced Statistical Computing - course notes by Robert Gray.
  • Robert M. Gray's web page with links to his downloadable books, e.g., Introduction to Statistical Signal Processing and Entropy and Information Theory.
  • Albert Tarantola's web page with links to his downloadable books, e.g., Inverse Problem Theory and Model Parameter Estimation.
  • Principles of Uncertainty by Joseph Kadane.
  • Bayesian Reasoning and Machine Learning by David Barber.
  • Advanced Data Analysis from an Elementary Point of View by Cosma Rohilla Shalizi.

 

Statistics-related blogs:

  • Statistical Modeling, Causal Inference, and Social Science by Andrew Gelman.
  • Xi'an's Og by Christian Robert.
  • Normal Deviate by Larry Wasserman.
  • Simply Statistics by Jeff Leek, Roger Peng and Rafael Irrizary.
